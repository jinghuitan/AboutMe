Swimming and Lifesaving

I started swimming more seriously after joining the Lifesaving CCA in poly. As a non-swimmer, I received guidance from dedicated coaches who helped me develop proper techniques, as well as lifesaving skills. Once I mastered the basics and approval from coaches, I progressed to earning certificates to become a qualified lifeguard:


  • Lifesaving 1, 2 and 3: The prerequisite for Bronze Medallion. It tests on basic water safety, land-based rescue and water rescue
  • Cardio Pulmonary Resuscitation (CPR): To provide practical experience in the lifesaving techniques of CPR and related emergency care.
  • Bronze Medallion: To establish an advanced standard of all round knowledge, skills and ability in aquatic rescue situations.

Being part of this CCA made me realise how much I enjoy swimming. It has become my favourite way to relax and clear my mind, and I always feel refreshed after a swim, no matter how my day goes.

Climbing

I began committing to my climbing journey, mainly on bouldering, in May 2024, and since then, I've been climbing regularly. I discovered the joy of bouldering through the sense of accomplishment I feel after clearing a route. Climbing challenges me both physically and mentally, each route requires problem-solving and pushing my limits, whether it's strength, technique, or perseverance. I enjoy the mental engagement as I figure out how to overcome the obstacles in front of me, while also improving my focus and determination. I've been sharing my climbs on Instagram @tofu.blok as a way to document my journey, giving me the chance to look back and reflect on how far I've come.


Fuelled by my love for climbing, I decided to apply as a service crew member at fit · bloc. This role has helped me develop strong communication skills through constant interaction with customers. It has also enhanced my flexibility and adaptability. Sometimes, it's not enough to follow the manual, as customers often have unique requests that require quick thinking and creative solutions.


Climbing has truly brought about a significant change in my life. I've never felt such eagerness to look forward to something before. It is now a part of my life that I can't live without.

Crocheting

Crocheting has become a beloved hobby of mine, offering the perfect blend of creativity and relaxation. I started with small projects like dolls' clothes and amigurumi, as I find it easier to commit to them since they don't take too long to finish. However, I hope to challenge myself with larger projects in the future.


One of my most significant projects is the sleeve I crocheted for my chalk bag. Tired of its original colour, I decided to add a custom sleeve. Inspired by Jellycat's toilet paper amusable, I recreated a design that now makes my chalk bag a unique and trusted climbing companion.


Crocheting also helps me unwind, as the repetitive motions are calming. It's always rewarding to transform a simple ball of yarn into something full of character and personality.
